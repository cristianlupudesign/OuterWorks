What we do

Structure, not clutter.

A good pergola isn't fussy — it's a clean timber frame that defines a seating area, throws a bit of dappled shade, and gives climbers something to grow up. Done right, it makes a garden feel designed rather than just filled.

We set the posts properly so the structure stands square and solid for years, and we match the timber and detailing to the fence and the rest of the garden. It's surveyed and priced before we start.

  • Posts set solid — concrete-footed and squared, not wobbling.
  • Clean timber detailing — tidy joints and a considered finish.
  • Sits with the fence — matched so it reads as one garden.
  • Works over deck or paving — planned around what's underneath.

Straight answers

Good to know about pergolas.

Does a pergola only work in a big garden?

No — a compact pergola often does more in a small garden, because it gives an otherwise plain space structure and a clear seating spot. We'll size it so it frames the area without crowding it.

Can it go over decking or a patio?

Yes, and that's the most common set-up. We plan the post positions around what's underneath — fixing down to a deck frame or setting footings through paving — so the structure is solid and the base stays sound. We'll work that out at the survey.

Will it take a climbing plant?

It can. If you want a rose, wisteria or vine growing over it, tell us and we'll build in the timber and spacing to support it as it fills out. A planted pergola looks better every year.
